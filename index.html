A Saturation-Aware Dual-Stage Framework for Intensity-Driven Adaptive PTW: Prioritizing Operational Safety and Site-Response Robustness in the Java-Sumatra Region

Hanif Andi Nugraha1* [ORCID], Dede Djuhana1 [ORCID], Adhi Harmoko Saputro1 [ORCID], Sigit Pramono2 [ORCID]
1 Department of Physics, FMIPA, Universitas Indonesia  |  2 BMKG, Jakarta, Indonesia
* Correspondence: hanif.andi@ui.ac.id

Abstract

The dataset contains 34,033 three-component accelerometric traces from 329 earthquakes (M 4.0-6.9) recorded at 388 stations across the Sunda subduction zone. We present a Saturation-Aware Dual-Stage framework that adaptively selects the P-wave time window (PTW) through a novel Feature Dichotomy paradigm. Stage 1 employs an XGBoost "Intensity Gate" classifier using the saturating parameter τc (51% importance) to route traces into three intensity classes (Weak, Felt, Damaging), while Stage 2 leverages the non-saturating CVAD (89.5% importance) to predict spectral acceleration Sa(T) across 103 periods (T = 0.1-5.0 s). The pipeline achieves a composite R² = 0.7309 and RMSE = 0.5181 log10 m/s² under fully operational single-station deployment.

Keywords: earthquake early warning, spectral acceleration, adaptive time window, XGBoost, feature dichotomy, Sunda subduction zone
